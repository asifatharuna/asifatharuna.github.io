Land Use Land Cover Classification (LULC)

Tools: R and ArcGIS (PRO)

Summary :

Land-use and land-cover maps have diverse applications in climate, environmental, agricultural, and socioeconomic studies. Hence, it is crucial to create accurate and reliable LULC maps. Traditional methods, such as ground surveys, are inefficient due to labor-intensive processes, high costs, and delays in map updates. A better alternative is remote sensing technology, which allows for repeated monitoring and extensive mapping using high-resolution imagery. Image classification, employing both supervised and unsupervised methods, is widely used for economically and efficiently generating LULC maps.

The objectives of this analysis are as follows:

  • To identify representative training and reference datasets.
  • To classify different LULC types within the study area using two supervised classification algorithms: Maximum Likelihood (ML) and Random Forest (RF).
  • To compare the performance of the implemented classifiers.

The analysis utilizes hyperspectral imagery, a digital object model (DOM) derived from airborne LiDAR point data, and a historic ATKIS LULC GIS layer to create high spatial resolution LULC maps over a large area. The aim is to enhance the separation of thematic classes and improve the map's quality by fusing DOM-derived object height information with hyperspectral imagery using principal component analysis. The guided unsupervised classification approach (Bauer et al., 1994) is then employed with the ATKIS GIS layer to stratify and mine representative training and test samples from the fused imagery. Finally, the Random Forest and Maximum likelihood machine learning classifier is used for the final classification, and accuracy assessment is conducted following procedures established in Olofsson et al., 2014.

The results indicate that the RF classifier outperforms the ML classifier, handling intra-class spectral variability and inter-class spectral similarities more efficiently.

Time Series Analysis & Pattern Recognition

Tools: R and ArcGIS

Summary :

The analysis focuses on utilizing time series satellite images to examine and characterize likely land-use and land-cover (LULC) changes in vegetated areas across Europe from 1983 to 2015, along with the potential underlying processes that triggered these changes. The Global Inventory Modeling and Mapping Studies (GIMMS) NDVI imagery time series data with an 8km spatial resolution and 15-day temporal resolution was used for this study. This dataset enabled the generation of phenological metrics used to characterize potential drivers that may have influenced the changes ground processes, following the criteria used in Stellmes et al., 2013, a method also known as the syndrome approach.

Tools: R and ArcGIS

Summary :

N fertilization is widely adopted for enhancing N supply and crop production (Koch et al., 2019), but excessive use may lead to economic and environmental challenges. The objectives of this analysis are as follows:

  • Radiometric correction of hyperspectral images from an unmanned aerial system (UAS).
  • Examining the sensitivity of different vegetation indices (VI) to N status in a potato field.
  • Quantifying the temporal and spatial variability of side dress N rate in the potato field based on measured canopy reflectance.

The study was conducted in Luxembourg, dividing the field into three random plots with different N treatments and three repetitions for each treatment across the crop season. Linear regression analysis between different hyperspectral spectral vegetation indices and the field observation was performed to find the best-performing index for assessing crop nitrogen concentration and quantifying sidedress nitrogen. The analysis results showcase the potential of hyperspectral remote sensing in monitoring nitrogen (N) status and determining optimal N fertilization rates in potato fields. The findings highlight the variability and site-specific nature of sidedress N, allowing for efficient N fertilization without compromising both yield and environmental considerations.
